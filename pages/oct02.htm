The beginning...

On October 24th, 2002,in the middle of chaos and problems, an amazing thing happened to us: Dylan was born.

At exactly 00:59am on the 24th, he came into this world, 12 weeks early. Karen (mum) had been to hell and back, having had major health problems with prancreatitus and gall stone problems, and Ric (dad) was trying to make sure she was well, as well as renovate the house they had recently bought together and work. During the morning of the 24th, she had gone to St. Georges hospital in Tooting to have ECP (the procedure where they put a fiber optic down the throat) to try and crush a gall stone which had been causing mum to be sick for the past two weeks solid.

Little Dylan lies in his incubator the day after his birth...the hand shown is of aunty susie.

The Birth...

The birth was quick - Karen was in the intensive care unit of Kingston Upon Thames hospital, when she experienced some pains just before midnight. A maternity nurse was on hand, checking the baby, when it was realised that the pains mum was having were contractions. They rushed her to the delivery unit and called dad.

Luckily we lived at the time in Roehampton, only a few moments from the hospital. By the time I arrived, she was being settled into one of the delivery suites, surrounded by seven nurses and doctors, including three from the Neo natal until, who had arrived together with incubator and other such accouterments...at 00:59am on the 24th, Dylan decided he wanted to check what life is all about, and made a quick entrance. The neonatal doctors quickly worked on him (god bless em) and within momements he was being whisked away. Dad is not ashamed to say that upon seeing his son born, he cried hysterically.
