The operation...

The beginning of December saw Karen have her gall bladder removed at last, although she was quite poorly for a few weeks after. By the time Dylan came home she was well on the way to recovery.....

Dylan was doing very well, and moved from the SCBU (the intensive baby care unit) to the next room, and in an open cot instead of an incubator..

Dylan's beautiful little hands....

He was still on assisted breathing, and he was still attempting to remove these tubes at every opportunity...

His milk was upped and he started to look healthier. The other baby Dylan was also here, in the next cot. He had progressed quicker than our Dylan, and was doing well although he was having trouble feeding, something Dylan (probably thanks to having an abundance of his fathers genes) certainly was not!


Dylan was still have Bradies and Apneas which was what the nurses were still keeping an eye out for, and why he was still hooked up to the monitoring equipment....

Dylan contemplates life outside....

The charity that helps raise money for the Neo Natal ward is called Born Too Soon, and they sell Christmas cards to help with this. The woman who runs the charity was very helpful, getting parking sorted out for free (it was costing a small fortune) and giving us advise and even free clothes for Dylan (it was very hard to find items so small).

Before coming home, Dylan was visited by some family and friends, including lola...

 

Dylan comes home....

On December 18th we were told something we never really expected to hear so soon - they were going to let us take home Dylan for Christmas!
We had to room in the hospital on the 21st and 22nd, which basically meant that we lived for two days in the hospital whilst we looked after Dylan full time. It was a small unremarkable room, with a nasty bed with plastic sheets (I guess they must have a lot of problems with bedwetting parents), but Dylan was as good as gold - sleeping and feeding. Somehow, we did not think it would be as easy as this....

Mum, thats not my bottle...

Christmas Day....

Dylans first Christmas was spent with his family and Grandma, Grandpa and aunty Donna. It was all a little too much for our Dylan, and he just snoozed his way through the day without a care in the world. As you would expect, lots of presents from friends and family were eagerly opened by mum (more please)...

Dylan relaxing wearing his Christmas outfit...

Talking politics with aunty Donna...

Whitstable...

Meanwhile, back in Whitstable dad, grandpa Tony and Mark had been busy finishing the house. It was not quite ready yet, but we had decided to move on the weekend of the 18th of January, the weekend before dad's 32nd birthday.

Lolo working very hard on mummy and daddy's room
